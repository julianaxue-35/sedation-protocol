Post-op sedation protocol: dogs, days 0 to 14

Decision support to prevent serotonin syndrome. For veterinary use only. Version 1.6.

Decision flowchart

Step 1
Patient assessment
Post-op day 0 to 14, requires sedation
Step 2
Screen for serotonergic drugs
If any present, trazodone cap drops from 10 to 7 mg/kg. See list below.
Step 3
Assess primary behaviour
Choose primary drug based on dominant behaviour
Step 4A · For fearful patient
Trazodone
  • Start 4 mg/kg PO
  • 1 to 2 h pre-procedure
  • Every 8 hours
  • Titrate +25% every 48 h
  • Reassess at each step
  • Max 10 mg/kg standalone
  • Max 7 mg/kg with serotonergic
If no response at max: add clonidine at starting dose
Step 4B · For aroused or reactive patient
Clonidine
  • Start 0.02 mg/kg PO
  • 2 h pre-procedure
  • Every 6 to 8 hours
  • Titrate +25% every 48 h
  • Reassess at each step
  • Max 0.05 mg/kg
  • No serotonin risk
If no response at max: add trazodone at starting dose
Combination rule. Add the other primary drug at its starting dose. Keep the original at its maximum during titration of the second. Only change one drug's dose at a time. If the second drug shows clinical effect, stop the original (no weaning needed if used 14 days or less).
Step 5 · Adjunct (optional, from day 1)
Gabapentin or pregabalin
Can be added alongside the primary drug from day 1, or added later if response is inadequate.
Gabapentin 10 mg/kg PO q8 to 12 h
Titrate +50% q48 h
Max 40 mg/kg
Pregabalin 2 mg/kg PO q8 to 12 h
Titrate +50% q48 h
Max 10 mg/kg
When changing one drug's dose, do not change another drug's dose simultaneously. You will not know which is producing the effect.
Step 6
Weaning
  • 14 days or less of use: no weaning required
  • More than 28 days of use: reduce by 25 to 50% each week
  • 14 to 28 days: clinical judgement
Step 7
Monitor for serotonin syndrome
Onset 30 min to 12 h. See recognition and emergency panels below.

Serotonergic drugs to screen for

These drug classes increase serotonin activity. Combining them with trazodone triggers the 7 mg/kg trazodone cap.

  • SARIs: trazodone (the protocol drug itself, listed for awareness)
  • SSRIs: fluoxetine, paroxetine, sertraline, citalopram
  • SNRIs: venlafaxine
  • TCAs: clomipramine, amitriptyline
  • MAOIs: selegiline
  • Opioid analgesics: tramadol
  • Antiemetics: ondansetron
  • Other: mirtazapine
  • Supplements: 5-HTP, St John's Wort

Recognise serotonin syndrome

  • CNS: agitation, restlessness, vocalisation, mydriasis, ataxia, hyperreflexia, tremors, seizures
  • Autonomic: hyperthermia, tachycardia or bradycardia, hypertension, hypersalivation, panting
  • GI: vomiting, diarrhoea, abdominal pain
  • Neuromuscular: muscle rigidity, fasciculation, shivering
  • Onset: 30 min to 12 h post-dose. Duration with treatment 24 to 72 h

If serotonin syndrome suspected

  • Stop all serotonergic drugs immediately
  • Cyproheptadine 1.1 mg/kg PO or per rectum every 4 to 6 h (crush and mix with saline if needed)
  • Alternative: chlorpromazine 0.2 to 0.5 mg/kg IV, IM or SC every 6 h
  • Supportive care: IV crystalloids 10 to 20 mL/kg bolus for hypovolaemia, active cooling for hyperthermia, methocarbamol for tremors
  • Seizures: diazepam or midazolam as indicated
  • Refer to 24-h emergency facility if severe or refractory
